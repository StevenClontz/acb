TheoremIII.3.2.2Linearity of Derivatives

The derivative of a sum (or difference, or scalar multiple) of functions is the same as the sum (or difference, or scalar multiple) of their derivatives.

That is, for any functions \(f(x), g(x)\) and any constant \(c\text{,}\) we have:

  1. \(\bigl( f(x) + g(x) \bigr)' = f'(x) + g'(x)\)
  2. \(\bigl( f(x) - g(x) \bigr)' = f'(x) - g'(x)\)
  3. \(\bigl( c\cdot f(x) \bigr)' = c \cdot f'(x).\)

This theorem allows us to take derivatives of sums, differences, and multiples simply by individually taking the derivative of each term.
