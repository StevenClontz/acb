Theorem3.2.3Derivatives of Exponential Functions

The derivative of any exponential function \(f(x) = e^{rx}\) is

\begin{equation*} f'(x) = r\, e^{rx}. \end{equation*}

In other words, to take the derivative of a natural exponental function (the base is the constant \(e\) and the exponent is a multiple of the variable \(rx\)), simply

  1. Multiply the expression by the rate constant \(r\text{.}\)
