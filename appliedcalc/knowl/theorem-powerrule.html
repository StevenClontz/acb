Theorem3.2.1The Power Rule for Derivatives

The derivative of any power function \(f(x) = x^n\) is

\begin{equation*} f'(x) = n\, x^{n-1}. \end{equation*}

In other words, to find the derivative of a function defined by a (constant) power of \(x\text{,}\) you can:

  1. Multiply the expression by the exponent, and then
  2. Decrease the exponent by one.
