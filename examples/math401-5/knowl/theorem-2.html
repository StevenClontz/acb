Theorem2.2Monotone convergence theorem

Let \(s_n\) be an increasing (respectively, decreasing) sequence. If \(s_n\) is bounded above (respectively, bounded below), then \(s_n\) is convergent and

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\to\infty} s_n \amp = \sup \{ s_n \} \amp\amp \text{(respectively,}\quad \inf \{ s_n \}.) \end{align*}

In other words, an increasing sequence which is bounded above will converge to its supremum; a decreasing sequence which is bounded above will converge to its infimum.
