Theorem3.3Cauchy sequences of rational numbers converge

Let \(s_n\) be a Cauchy sequence of rational numbers. Then \(s_n\) is a convergent sequence, and there exists a real number \(L \in\mathbb{R}\) such that \(L=\lim_{n\to\infty} s_n\text{.}\)

(Note, here \(L\) is a real number in the sense of the completeness axiom, that is, it is the least upper bound of some set of rational numbers.)
